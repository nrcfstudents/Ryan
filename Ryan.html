    Dear Steve and Judy Lewis.

    I hope this message finds you well. As for me, I am doing well with my family. Life in high school is good, as I have been learning new subjects and playing games. My favorite subject in high school is biology. I also enjoy playing rugby, but I haven't joined the team yet because the other players are much taller than me.


    I attend an extra county school that provides us with all the facilities we need to excel in our studies and sports. Our teachers are professionals in their fields and they provide us with the right content to help us succeed in our exams. Our school is also strict on discipline so that the students can perform well in their studies. NRCF has been very helpful to me. They have provided me with computer training, which has enabled me to use that knowledge to answer questions on my school exams. Thank you for your support and may God bless you.

    Yours faithfully,

    Ryan Karanja.

    Dear Steve and Judy Lewis

    Hey, I hope you and your family are doing great. My family and I are doing well too. I just completed my primary school level and achieved 308 marks in the National exam test (KCPE). I'm really proud of my performance, and I'm eagerly anticipating the opportunity to join a better secondary school in Kenya.

    During this Christmas holiday, I got a chance to visit my rural village and undergo circumcision, a cultural and traditional practice that holds significance in our community. The experience allowed me to connect with my roots and participate in a ritual that has been passed down through generations. As I went through the process, I gained a deeper understanding of the cultural values and customs that shape our community. It was a unique and meaningful aspect of my holiday that make up our village life.

    This Christmas holiday, I am celebrating it in the rural area. I wanted to take a moment to wish you a Merry Christmas and a Happy New Year! I hope your festive season is filled with joy, warmth, and wonderful moments.

    Take care, and goodbye for now.

    Yours Ryan Karanja
